# UX / UI Inspiration Board

Research date: 2026-03-19

## North-star direction

RestShore should feel like:
- calm but not bland
- editorial but not precious
- clinically serious but not cold
- premium but not overdesigned
- guided but not constrained

The best model is a blend of:
- Stripe-level trust and hierarchy
- Linear-level clarity and speed
- Notion-level modular organization
- Framer-level motion and immediacy
- Calm/Headspace-level emotional softness

## Marketing site inspiration

### Stripe
Why it matters:
- crisp hierarchy
- strong product trust
- clear conversion path
- premium spacing and confidence

What to borrow:
- bold headline + short proof line
- one primary CTA
- clean feature sections
- polished social proof and trust cues

### Linear
Why it matters:
- fast comprehension
- precise language
- productiveness without clutter
- motion that supports meaning

What to borrow:
- concise labels
- status clarity
- use of "progress as a product"
- sharp typography and restrained color

### Framer
Why it matters:
- interactive, premium, and playful
- motion is part of the story
- visual hierarchy feels alive

What to borrow:
- animated states
- gradient or glass-like treatment
- section transitions that feel intentional
- polished bento cards and live previews

### Notion
Why it matters:
- information architecture feels calm and modular
- complex content can still feel approachable

What to borrow:
- modular panels
- clear section naming
- docs-like clarity for summaries and reports
- "workspaces" for plan, logs, and doctor-facing data

### Calm and Headspace
Why it matters:
- emotional reassurance
- warm bedtime feel
- simple onboarding

What to borrow:
- softer palettes
- gentle illustration or iconography
- soothing microcopy
- content library feeling for bedtime support

## Product surface inspiration

### Intake and check-ins
The best pattern is a narrow focus:
- one question at a time
- minimal header chrome
- clear progress
- visible reason to answer
- no clutter above the fold

RestShore should feel more like a guided conversation than a form.

### Calendar creation wow moment
This needs to feel like a live build, not a progress bar.

Better cues:
- stage names
- live feed of what is being added
- "now personalizing" explanation
- visible milestones
- optimistic motion even before the server finishes the next batch

### Report and summary pages
These should feel editorial and usable:
- hero summary
- key metrics in cards
- plan summary
- doctor-ready section
- what changed and why
- next action clearly visible

### Clinician-facing escalation page
This should feel like a compact clinical handoff:
- primary complaint
- duration
- current pattern
- contributing factors
- adherence / diary consistency
- red flags
- concise recommendation summary

## Visual language recommendations

- Use a warm neutral base with one cool accent.
- Keep typography expressive on headlines and highly legible in body text.
- Make labels and progress feel precise.
- Use motion sparingly, but let it communicate momentum.
- Avoid generic wellness purple-on-white aesthetics.
- Prefer editorial cards, bento layouts, and layered surfaces.
- Use color to encode meaning, not decoration.

## Layout principles for future screens

- Marketing home: big promise, concise proof, one obvious CTA.
- Dashboard: "what matters now" first, deeper data second.
- Summary page: clear narrative of what happened and what to do next.
- Doctor report: structured, concise, printable, and shareable.
- Check-in: mobile-first, low friction, tiny cognitive load.
