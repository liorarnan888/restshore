# Content Policy and Governance

Research date: 2026-03-19

## Ownership

The product-content function owns:
- CBT-I content policy
- tone and messaging consistency
- competitor research
- user pain understanding
- SEO-aware messaging
- user-facing wording standards

## Therapeutic content policy

RestShore should always treat CBT-I as the core method.

Allowed:
- clear behavioral guidance
- personalization based on the user's own answers
- educational explanations of why a rule matters
- pattern-based plan adjustments
- doctor-ready summaries for escalation

Not allowed:
- claiming to cure insomnia
- implying one night can diagnose the whole problem
- overreacting to a single bad night
- writing content that shames or blames
- making sleep hygiene sound like the full treatment

## Update policy

### What can change after one logged night
- the title of the sleep event
- the sleep event times, if the user reported different actual times
- the sleep event description, including logged outcome
- the user-facing summary of that night

### What should not change after one logged night
- the future treatment plan
- the overall 6-week structure
- the sleep window target, unless a repeated pattern appears

### When future changes are allowed
- only after repeated patterns
- only after enough recent evidence
- only within the defined rule table

## Safety and escalation policy

Escalate or soften the flow when the user shows possible signs of:
- untreated sleep apnea
- excessive daytime sleepiness
- bipolar / mania risk
- seizures
- pregnancy-related special handling
- high fall risk
- acute mental health concerns
- substance-related sleep disruption
- shift-work complexity

When in doubt:
- recommend clinician review
- preserve the user's history
- do not overstate certainty

## Governance process

Any content change should be checked against:
- the CBT-I treatment model
- user clarity
- tone consistency
- SEO impact
- clinician-facing clarity
- testing implications in the Test Center

## Future artifacts this policy supports

- clinician escalation summary page
- marketing site messaging
- dashboard copy system
- email templates
- report and check-in patterns
- content QA checklist

## Practical review checklist

Before shipping a new content surface, ask:
- Does it explain the "why" clearly?
- Does it sound like RestShore?
- Does it overpromise?
- Does it shame the user?
- Does it fit CBT-I principles?
- Does it still make sense if the user only skims it?
- Would a clinician find the language credible?
