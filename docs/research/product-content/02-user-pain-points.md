# User Pain Points

Research date: 2026-03-19

## Executive summary

People with insomnia are not only trying to "sleep better." They are usually trying to restore control, confidence, and predictability.

The product should understand that the user may be:
- tired of trying random tips
- skeptical of another sleep app
- worried about doing the "wrong" thing
- ashamed that sleep is still hard
- afraid of one bad night derailing everything
- exhausted by reminders
- unable to remember exactly what happened during the night

## Core emotional pain

### Frustration
Users are often stuck in a loop of effort without payoff. That creates anger, helplessness, and fatigue.

### Loss of confidence
After repeated bad nights, users do not trust their own judgment about what helped and what did not.

### Fear of failure
If the product feels strict, users may assume they are failing the treatment instead of learning from it.

### Overwhelm
Long questionnaires, too many reminders, or too many rules can make users disengage.

### Shame
Some users feel broken, weak, or "bad at sleep." The content has to avoid reinforcing that.

## What users are actually trying to do

- understand why sleep is hard
- know what to do tonight
- keep the plan simple enough to follow
- see whether the plan is working
- avoid making sleep worse after a bad night
- know when to escalate to a clinician

## Common friction points by journey stage

### Intake
- too many questions
- questions that feel repetitive
- unclear reason for asking personal details
- too little sense of payoff

### Nighttime
- worry loops
- clock-checking
- uncertainty about what to do if awake
- temptation to improvise

### Morning
- poor recall of what actually happened
- reluctance to enter details
- confusion about whether to "report a bad night" or "wait and see"

### Week-to-week
- desire to overreact to one bad night
- unclear whether the plan should change yet
- need for visible progress and reassurance

### Escalation
- concern that the user may need a clinician
- uncertainty about how to summarize the problem clearly

## What users need from RestShore

- one clear next step
- short and specific instructions
- a sense that the system knows what to do with their answers
- a calm explanation of why the current recommendation exists
- visible respect for their time
- a feeling that the product is a partner, not a judge

## What to say more often

- "You are not starting over."
- "One rough night does not reset the plan."
- "We only change the future when the pattern says to."
- "This is meant to be manageable."
- "We will keep the history intact."

## What to avoid saying

- "Just stick to it"
- "You should know better"
- "This is simple"
- "You failed"
- "If you were disciplined, this would work"

## Product implications

- Use short flows with a clear reason to answer each question.
- Make logging easier than remembering.
- Make recovery instructions obvious.
- Give the user a feeling of accumulating progress.
- Surface summary pages that explain what changed and why.
- Avoid reminder sprawl; one reliable entry point is better than many noisy ones.
