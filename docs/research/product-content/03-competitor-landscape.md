# Competitor Landscape

Research date: 2026-03-19

## Executive summary

The market splits into a few distinct patterns:
- clinical CBT-I products with structured weekly progression
- support-heavy digital clinics with clinicians/coaches
- meditation and sleep-content brands with premium emotional polish

RestShore can differentiate by combining:
- real CBT-I depth
- beautiful premium UX
- daily adaptation
- doctor-ready summaries
- a calendar-native behavior change model

## Competitive matrix

| Product | Positioning | Core shape | Strengths | Gaps / risks | What RestShore should learn |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Sleepio | 6-week CBT-I digital therapeutic | Weekly sessions, diary, reminders, community, articles | Strong structure, clear CBT-I framing, evidence-forward, personalized by onboarding and progress | Can feel programmatic and clinical; less premium emotional design | Use the 6-week structure, diary cadence, and clear personalization while making the experience warmer and more cinematic |

| Headspace Sleep | Sleep/wellness content ecosystem | Sleepcasts, wind-downs, guided meditations, SOS, sleep music | Strong brand trust, soothing UX, good bedtime guidance | Again, not CBT-I; less structured treatment logic | Borrow the bedtime calm, guided routines, and low-friction entry into sleep support |

## What each competitor does especially well

### Sleepio
- makes CBT-I feel like a guided course
- uses 6 weekly sessions, diary, reminders, and a support library
- presents itself as structured, consistent, and science-backed

### Somryst
- frames insomnia as treatable with a real protocol
- gives clinicians visibility through a dashboard
- makes the diary and sleep restriction central, not optional

### CBT-i Coach and Insomnia Coach
- make the treatment components visible to the user
- reinforce diaries, routines, and relaxation
- keep the focus on sleep behaviors rather than generic wellness

### Sleep Reset
- wins on trust, service depth, and clinician language
- makes the sleep problem feel serious and worth expert support
- presents a strong escalation pathway

### Calm and Headspace
- win on mood, polish, and low-friction emotional appeal
- make sleep feel inviting instead of clinical
- create strong content ecosystems around bedtime

## Market whitespace for RestShore

RestShore can own the intersection of:
- CBT-I rigor
- consumer-grade beauty
- personalized calendar behavior change
- daily adaptation
- doctor-ready summaries
- plain-language explanation of why each action matters

This combination is not fully owned by any one competitor.

## Product strategy implications

- Use the structured confidence of Sleepio and Somryst.
- Use the visual warmth and emotional calm of Calm and Headspace.
- Use the practical diary/rule behavior language from VA apps.
- Use the trust and escalation framing of Sleep Reset.
- Make the calendar a central behavior-change object, not just a notification surface.
