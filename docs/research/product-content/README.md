# Product Content Research

Research date: 2026-03-19

This folder is the source of truth for RestShore product content strategy.
It covers:
- CBT-I treatment mechanics and success factors
- user pain points and behavior patterns
- competitor landscape
- UX/UI inspiration for current and future surfaces
- voice, tone, messaging, and SEO guidance
- content policy and governance

## How to use these files

- Start with `01-cbti-deep-dive.md` for the treatment model.
- Read `02-user-pain-points.md` to understand the emotional and practical experience.
- Use `03-competitor-landscape.md` to see where the market is crowded and where RestShore can differentiate.
- Use `04-ux-ui-inspiration.md` when designing marketing pages, dashboard surfaces, reports, and clinician handoff experiences.
- Use `05-voice-seo-guidelines.md` for product copy, landing pages, and search strategy.
- Use `06-content-policy-and-governance.md` before writing any new user-facing content or therapeutic flows.
- Use `07-phase-3-structural-adaptation.md` for weekly structural plan changes based on diary patterns.

## Executive takeaways

- CBT-I should be framed as the core treatment model, not a loose collection of sleep tips.
- RestShore should feel clinically grounded, emotionally warm, and visually premium at the same time.
- The product should be specific and behaviorally actionable: what to do tonight, what changed, and why.
- The language must stay consistent across intake, report, calendar, email, and future public pages.
- Competitive products show two strong patterns:
  - clinical rigor, diary-driven structure, and trust
  - polished emotional UX, motion, and premium marketing
- RestShore should combine both.
